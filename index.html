Exploring Major Religions Beliefs

Today's blog gives a brief overview of four major religions: Christianity, Hinduism, Sikhism, and Islam. We explore their core beliefs and philosophies, from Christianity’s focus on faith in Jesus Christ, to Hinduism’s concepts of karma and moksha, Sikhism’s emphasis on equality and service, and Islam’s devotion to Allah through the teachings of the Prophet Muhammad.

Christianity is a monotheistic religion based on the teachings of Jesus Christ. It emphasizes love, forgiveness, and salvation through faith in Christ. Christians follow the Bible, focusing on the New Testament.

Sikhism, founded by Guru Nanak in the 15th century, teaches belief in one God, equality, and selfless service. Core practices include meditation, community service (Seva), and living an honest life.

Hinduism, one of the oldest religions, believes in Brahman as the supreme spirit. It teaches karma (action), dharma (duty), and moksha (liberation). The religion honors many gods, including Vishnu, Shiva, and Devi, with practices like yoga and meditation for spiritual growth.

Islam is a monotheistic religion founded on the teachings of Prophet Muhammad. Followers believe in one God (Allah) and follow the Quran. Key practices include the Five Pillars: faith, prayer, charity, fasting, and pilgrimage.
